Campaign Premise

Echoes beneath the Sand

Welcome to Shar'Mazir, a bustling border-city at the northern edge of the unforgiving desert of Sharras'Thar. You are newly minted members of the Sandguard, a Freelance Order of guides, explorers, and protectors tasked with charting the desert's shifting sands, escorting caravans, and facing the beasts that lurk in the dunes. Your first missions may seem routine, but soon, whispers of a forgotten power will lead you into something far darker than any of you expected.
The city's ruling Council of Cliffs — an uneasy alliance of ancient factions — controls everything in Shar'Mazir: from water flow and the trade routes to the protection of its walls. But recent discoveries suggest that greed and ambition have led to corruption at its highest levels, risking not only the city's stability but also the balance of the desert itself. It seems that ancient relics are surfacing, strange sandstorms have become more frequent, and desert cultists speak of a forgotten one awakening.
As a Sandguard member, you're not only protecting the people but are about to find yourself in a battle against an ancient force once sealed in the desert's depths. Dark forces stir as you navigate conspiracies in the city, face sand-dwelling monstrosities, and unravel the secrets buried beneath the sands. With each mission, the danger grows, and you'll be forced to make choices that could save — or doom — not only Shar'Mazir but all of the surrounding Region of Kharashan.

Are you ready to brave the desert, uncover the truth, and face the Echoes Beneath the Dunes?

Ancient Language of Desharim

Hundreds or thousands of years before the current day, the continent of Desharim was once controlled by a single Empire of Nomads with a distinct Language. Eventhough it is not actively taught or spoken anymore, some of the areas more connected to the heart of Desharim, meaning the main desert of Sharras'Thar, still use fragments of the Ancient Language and try to keep its memory alive through documents, shards of ancient texts and maps.
